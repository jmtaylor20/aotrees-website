When a Tree Becomes a Risk

Dead and dangerous trees can fall without much warning. A tree may look stable from a distance while the inside is weak, hollow, diseased, storm-damaged, or cracked.

If a tree is leaning toward your home, dropping large limbs, showing signs of decay, or sitting too close to structures, it needs a careful look from an experienced tree service.

Signs You May Need Dangerous Tree Removal

Do not ignore a tree that looks unsafe. These warning signs can point to a tree that needs attention.

Dead Limbs

Large dead limbs can break loose and damage roofs, vehicles, fences, or people below.

Leaning Trees

A tree leaning toward a home, driveway, road, or utility line can become a serious property risk.

Rot or Hollow Areas

Soft spots, cavities, fungus, and hollow trunks may show internal decay or weakened structure.

Storm Damage

High winds and heavy rain can split trunks, break limbs, and weaken root systems.

Cracks or Splits

Cracks in the trunk or large limbs may signal instability and increased risk of failure.

Too Close to Structures

Trees near homes, buildings, fences, power lines, and driveways require careful planning.
